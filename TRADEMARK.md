The **Westport Partners** name and the Westport Partners logo (the mountain-range
mark), and the **Relay** project name, are marks of Westport Partners.

> Note: trademark registration status is being confirmed. Regardless of registration,
> Westport Partners asserts common-law rights in these marks. This document describes
> acceptable use.

## What the license does and doesn't cover

Relay's source code is licensed under the **Apache License, Version 2.0** (see `LICENSE`).
The Apache-2.0 license grants you broad rights to use, modify, and redistribute the
**code** — but, per section 6 of that license, it does **not** grant any rights to use
Westport Partners' **trademarks, names, or logos**. Those are covered here.

## Acceptable use (no permission needed)

- Stating, factually, that your project "uses Relay" or "is built on Relay."
- Referring to the project by name in documentation, articles, talks, and discussions.
- Keeping the existing Westport Partners attribution and logo in unmodified copies.

## Uses that need written permission

- Using the Westport Partners name or logo in a way that implies sponsorship,
  endorsement, partnership, or affiliation that does not exist.
- Using the marks as (or as part of) the name of your own product, service, company,
  or a fork that could be confused with the official project.
- Modifying the logo, or using it to brand a derivative distribution.

## Forks and derivatives

You are free to fork and modify the code under Apache-2.0. If you distribute a modified
version, please **remove the Westport Partners logo and branding** from your distribution
(or clearly mark it as an unofficial fork) so users aren't misled about its origin. You
may, of course, retain the attribution required by the license and NOTICE file.
